Small teams don't need enterprise project management — they need something that keeps tasks from falling through the cracks without a lot of setup overhead. Here's how three popular options stack up.

## ClickUp: best if you want one tool to replace several

ClickUp's pitch is breadth: tasks, docs, goals, and chat in one workspace, with AI features layered on top for summarizing threads and drafting updates. The tradeoff is that its sheer number of features can mean more setup time up front than a small team may want to spend.

## Notion: best if your team already lives in docs

Notion started as a docs-and-wiki tool and grew project-management features on top, with an AI assistant that helps draft and summarize content. Teams that already organize their thinking in written docs tend to find Notion the most natural fit, since tasks live alongside the writing rather than in a separate silo.

## Asana: best for straightforward task and timeline tracking

Asana is more purpose-built around classic project management: tasks, timelines, dependencies, workload views. If your team's main need is "who's doing what by when" without a lot of extra document/wiki functionality, it's worth a look as the more focused option of the three.

## Quick comparison

| Priority | Better fit |
|---|---|
| All-in-one workspace (docs + tasks + chat) | ClickUp |
| Docs-first team that wants tasks alongside notes | Notion |
| Clean, focused task/timeline tracking | Asana |

As with any SaaS comparison, confirm current pricing tiers and AI-feature availability directly on each vendor's site, since these change more often than review articles get updated.

## The bottom line

For a small team, the fastest way to decide is to pick the one whose free tier most closely matches how you already think about work — as documents, as tasks, or as timelines — and try running one real project through it before committing.
