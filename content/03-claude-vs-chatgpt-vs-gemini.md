There's no single "best" general AI assistant — the right pick depends on what you're actually using it for day to day. Here's a grounded, non-hype comparison for people deciding where to spend a subscription.

## Claude: strong for long documents, coding, and careful writing

Claude tends to stand out for working with long documents and files, for coding tasks (including agentic tools that can act semi-independently in a codebase), and for writing that needs a careful, less "AI-sounding" tone. If your work involves reviewing contracts, working through long reports, or building software, it's a strong default.

## ChatGPT: broad ecosystem and plugin/app integrations

ChatGPT has the largest user base and the broadest set of third-party integrations and custom GPTs. If you want an assistant with the widest range of pre-built integrations and community tooling, it's worth strong consideration for that reason alone.

## Gemini: tight integration with Google's ecosystem

Gemini's advantage is how deeply it's woven into Google Workspace — Docs, Sheets, Gmail. If your business already runs on Google Workspace, the in-context AI features there can be the most frictionless of the three to adopt.

## How to choose without overthinking it

Most people are better served by trying the free tier of each on a real task from their own work — a contract to review, an email to draft, a spreadsheet to clean up — than by reading a spec comparison. Subscription costs and available models change frequently across all three providers, so check current plans directly on each provider's pricing page rather than trusting numbers from an older article.

## The bottom line

If you had to pick one to start with for document-heavy or coding-heavy freelance work, Claude is a reasonable default; for the widest plugin ecosystem, ChatGPT; for Google Workspace-native teams, Gemini. Many power users end up keeping more than one subscription rather than forcing a single choice.
